Why Global 1776

1776 was not merely the birth of a nation. It was the articulation of a principle: that legitimate authority derives from the consent of the governed and that no government, corporation, institution, or technological system stands above the inherent rights of humanity.

Global 1776 extends that principle into the modern age.

The Eight Founding Principles

★
●

Human Liberty

Every individual possesses rights that exist independent of governments or institutions.

○

Free Expression

Speech must remain free even when ideas are unpopular.

♙

Bodily Autonomy

Every person retains sovereignty over their own body and medical decisions.

⚖

Due Process

No individual shall be deprived of liberty without transparent and impartial process.

⌂

Property Rights

The fruits of labor and lawful ownership deserve protection.

◉

Transparency

Power exercised in secret becomes power unaccountable.

▣

Accountability

Governments, corporations, and institutions must answer to those they affect.

♟

Self-Governance

Communities and nations retain the right to determine their own future.

What We Reject

We reject any system or ideology that relies on:

  • Censorship
  • Coercion
  • Corruption
  • Surveillance Abuse
  • Arbitrary Rule
  • Manufactured Dependency
  • Concentrated Unaccountable Power

Declaration Excerpt

We affirm that liberty is not a privilege bestowed by rulers but an inherent condition of human existence.

We affirm that power derives legitimacy only through the consent of the governed.

We affirm that future generations deserve freedom equal to or greater than our own.
