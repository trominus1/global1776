21 Articles of Human Sovereignty

A modern charter affirming that rights belong to human beings—not governments, corporations, institutions, algorithms, or machines.

Preamble

We hold that every human being is born with inherent rights. These rights are not granted by institutions. They precede institutions. They limit institutions. They must be protected from every concentration of power, whether public, private, digital, financial, medical, ideological, or technological.

Article I — Freedom of Expression

Every person has the right to think, speak, publish, debate, question, and dissent without censorship or compelled belief.

Article II — Bodily Autonomy

The human body is sovereign. No medical intervention, testing, treatment, medication, or experiment shall occur without informed and voluntary consent.

Article III — Privacy & Data Rights

Every person has the right to privacy, data ownership, and freedom from surveillance systems that monitor, score, manipulate, or control daily life.

Article IV — Transparent Governance

Power must be visible. Government secrecy must be narrow, temporary, and accountable to the people.

Article V — Equal Justice

Justice must be uniform. No person or institution stands above the law.

Article VI — Food Sovereignty

Every person has the right to grow, choose, share, and consume natural food without coercion, deception, or forced dependency.

Article VII — Healthcare Freedom

Medicine must serve healing, consent, transparency, and human dignity.

Article VIII — Family Sovereignty

Families hold a primary role in the care, education, formation, and protection of children.

Article IX — Education Freedom

Education must cultivate reason, conscience, literacy, skill, and independent thought—not obedience to ideological authority.

Article X — Sound Money

Monetary systems must serve people, not trap nations and families in permanent dependency.

Article XI — Property Rights

Property, labor, invention, and the fruits of honest work shall not be seized or controlled without due process.

Article XII — Limited Government

Government exists to secure rights. It may not become the master of the people it was created to serve.

Article XIII — Environmental Stewardship

Air, water, soil, and food systems must be protected through transparency, accountability, and responsible stewardship.

Article XIV — Technology as Servant

Technology must remain a tool of human flourishing, never a master over human liberty.

Article XV — Algorithmic Accountability

No algorithm or artificial intelligence system shall secretly determine the rights, opportunities, access, or status of a human being.

Article XVI — Freedom of Belief

No person shall be compelled to adopt any religion, ideology, worldview, or doctrine by state, corporation, school, platform, or institution.

Article XVII — Freedom of Association

People have the right to gather, organize, build communities, and peacefully resist abuses of power.

Article XVIII — Economic Freedom

Commerce should serve human flourishing, honest exchange, fair opportunity, and local resilience.

Article XIX — Peace

War must never become the business model of the powerful or the burden of the innocent.

Article XX — Public Accountability

Every institution exercising power over people must be answerable to people.

Article XXI — Human Sovereignty

Rights belong to human beings. No government, corporation, institution, algorithm, or machine may lawfully claim authority above them.
